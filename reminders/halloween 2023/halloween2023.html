Lucía Otero
Optimizing the secondary use of outpatient data in Spain
Lucía Otero is an alumni from the Cumming School of Medicine. Lucía spent approximately 3 years at the Centre for Health Informatics, working as a research assistant and studying a Masters degree in Health Services Research. The focus of her thesis was the development of international indicators for assessing the quality of administrative ICD-coded health data.
She currently works as a methodologist at the Research Unit of the Spanish Society of Rheumatology. In her role, she is responsible for: study design and methodology, project coordination, data analysis, as well as scientific dissemination. Her work focuses on pharmacovigilance, drug effectiveness and data quality, using data from nationwide prospective registries and patient-reported outcomes.
Join us at the Centre for Health Informatics for an in-depth chat about optimizing the secondary use of outpatient data in Spain.
