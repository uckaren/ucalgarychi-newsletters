Websites start at £1,500 and depending on what you need, can range up to £10,000. That might sound like a lot, and it is, but if you have a business, your website can be crucial to it’s success.

Here’s how a website project usually runs:
1 Content Strategy
We will have an initial call or meeting to discuss what it is that you’re looking for, and work out why your current solution is performing. We’ll then make notes and I’ll give you some things to think about before our Strategy Session. In this session we will dig deep in to the goals of your website, how we will measure and define success, review our research, align on a visual direction, and plan exactly what content we need on the site and how we will structure it.
2 Wire Framing
This is where I will structure the framework of the website and we will work together to refine it until we are happy with the layout and content architecture. This is like the blue print of the website, no specific design will be done here (so no font choices, colours etc.).
3 Design
Now we will work up the high fidelity design of your website - the bells and whistles so to speak. We will be basing the design decisions on our research and planning from our content strategy session. Once we’re happy with how the site looks, we move on to the development.
4 Development
The website will be created in Webflow. I’ll share links as I go via a subdomain (for example www.tompiggott.subdomain.com). This means that we won’t disrupt your current site and we can test the site before going live. The site will be fully responsive, meaning that it will adapt to fit all screen sizes. You will be able to see this every step of the way.
Once we’ve finished all of the above, we’ll have a meeting where I will transfer the website over to you, and teach you how to edit it yourself. Don’t worry, it’s really simple. I will also record training videos for you to refer back to at any point!
