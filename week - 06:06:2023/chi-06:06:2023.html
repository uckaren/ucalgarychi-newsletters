call for manuscripts

JPDS is delighted to present this new call for manuscripts for the new special collection of Notes on Population Data.

The collection will consist of articles that allow researchers and practitioners to report on unexpected or underestimated data issues they encountered during their population data science projects. These can be any of the aspects of how data was collected, recorded, sampled, captured, processed, standardized, cleaned, transformed, linked, integrated, and merged/fused. Often no or only very limited details of these processes and tasks are described in journal publications. As a result, lessons learnt in data aspects are not shared with the community.

The emphasis of a Data Note should not be on the description of a data set but rather on the process of what has been done with this data from its source until it was research-ready. The readers should learn what kind of unexpected technical or organisational problems were encountered, and if or how these problems could be solved.

Data Science Research Day
UofC data science research day, is a full-day event on June 15th at the MacEwan Ballroom on main campus.
As part of the new transdisciplinary scholarship initiative led by the office of the Vice President Research at the University of Calgary, the data science research day aims to bring together data scientists and AI researchers from all across the campus and establish new connections and collaborations. We will have an exciting day with invited presentations from faculty members across the whole campus.
Additionally, there will be a poster session in the afternoon where students and postdoctoral fellows can present their work as a way to give all data science groups the opportunity to present their research. Students and postdocs can simply indicate their interest presenting a poster while signing up for this event.
