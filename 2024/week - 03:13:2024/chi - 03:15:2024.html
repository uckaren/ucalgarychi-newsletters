2024 Interdisciplinary Student Showcase: Scientific Pluralism

The Future is in Great Hands! f you joined us last year, then you know what this is all about! Our students and trainees put a lot of hard work into every year of their program and we want to honour their efforts through this annual, student-focused forum. This is an opportunity for students and trainees to showcase their work, practice presenting through different modalities, learn new or fortify current skills, and build networking capacity. You are invited to join us on Monday, April 29, 2024, to check out some amazing student work and see what challenges future leaders in public health are working on resolving!

This showcase will highlight student and trainee work spanning across all programs within Community Health Sciences. This includes two undergraduate programs (Health and Society Major (Bachelor of Health Sciences) and Bachelor of Community Rehabilitation), two graduate programs (MSc and PhD, and a Masterâ€™s in Health Data Science and Biostatistics), a residency training program in Public Health and Preventive Medicine and Postdoctoral Fellows.

This event is open to students, faculty, and staff as attendees. Students and trainees from the programs listed above are invited to partake in any or all of the 3 presentation activities which include: Ignite Talks, 3-Minute Thesis speed rounds, and poster presentations with guided poster walks.

To learn more visit this page. If you have any question please email mnroe@ucalgary.ca.

UCSafety app for mobile emergency alerts now also available for guests

The UCSafety app is now the only app used by UCalgary to push out mobile alerts in case of an emergency on campus. Users with UCalgary credentials (students, postdocs, faculty, and staff) already have access to UCSafety and all of its features. We are happy to share that guests and limited-term associates can now also use a guest version of the UCSafety app that provides emergency alerts. Downloading the UCSafety app is quick and easy.
