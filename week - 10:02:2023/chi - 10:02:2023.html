emergency management - fire drills

This is a reminder that, as part of our emergency preparedness training, the university will be conducting its Annual Evacuation Drills for all buildings on campus between September 11 – 20, 2023. The date and time for CWPHB is September 13th, 2023 at 1:45pm.

These drills are scheduled twice per day in different buildings and typically take 15 minutes to complete. Please refer to the 2023 drill schedule to see when your building is scheduled for evacuation. The drills are a critical exercise to improve our preparedness and response in the case of an emergency on campus.

To ensure the safety of ourselves, as well as our students, faculty and staff, everyone has responsibilities to ensure their own safety and is required to follow all evacuation protocols, including:

  • evacuating a building once the building fire alarm is activated;
  • not entering a building that is in alarm.
  • Instructors, managers and supervisors must review the Evacuation Instructions with their students and staff as part of the annual hazard assessments and worksite safety orientations
  • This ensures that everyone is prepared to evacuate the building upon alarm.

    National Day for Truth and Reconciliation
    On September 29, 2023, UCalgary’s Office of Indigenous Engagement, in partnership with Calgary Public Library, will honour National Day for Truth and Reconciliation in Canada / Orange Shirt Day with special events held on campus and at Central Library.

    The events are intended to raise awareness about the impact and legacy of the Indian Residential School System in Canada and bring people together in the spirit of reconciliation.

    The events are intended to raise awareness about the impact and legacy of the Indian Residential School System in Canada and bring people together in the spirit of reconciliation.

    National Day for Truth and Reconciliation in Canada / Orange Shirt Day is an opportunity to learn about the history of Residential Schools, intergenerational trauma and Indigenous resilience. This day aligns with the time of year when Indigenous children were forcibly taken from their homes to attend Residential Schools. Many did not return home, many were traumatized for life and some have been brave enough to share their stories. Through the acknowledgement of truth and increased awareness, we aspire to become a country where “Every Child Matters.”
